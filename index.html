📖 Today's Concept: Dollar Cost Averaging

Dollar cost averaging is a way of investing where you put a fixed amount of money into a stock or fund at regular intervals, no matter what the price is. This helps reduce the impact of market ups and downs on your investments. By doing this, you're spreading out your investments over time, which can help you avoid making big purchases when prices are high. It's a strategy that helps you invest steadily and consistently, without trying to time the market.

Think of it like: Think of it like buying groceries - you don't stop buying food just because the price of your favorite snack went up, you keep buying it regularly because you need it. Similarly, with dollar cost averaging, you keep investing a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the market's performance.
💬 Remember: Dollar cost averaging helps you invest steadily and avoid trying to time the market, reducing the risk of making big purchases when prices are high.
